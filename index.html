What is Augmented Reality?

Augmented reality (AR) is the combination of a real-world environment and a virtual environment. A user’s view of the physical world is supplemented with a computer-generated visual and audio input.

Augmented Reality (AR)
Virtual Reality (VR)
Overlays information onto the existing environment using real-time audio and visual signals Uses three dimensional computer-generated environments in which a user is fully immersed and can explore and interact with the environment
Users are able to distinguish between reality and overlaid information User can only see virtual environment

Uses of Augmented Reality

Augmented reality can be used in many scenarios such as navigation overlays, remote surgeries, and military training, while AR smartphone applications can be used in GPS systems as well as object recognition. The Augmented Reality Market also includes the Virtual Reality Market. Applications for virtual reality include the gaming and medical industries.

Benefits of using Augmented Reality

Augmented Reality has the ability to shape the world. Some examples of beneifts include:

1. Hands-free - For users who need to constantly be on the move while performing tasks, augmented reality devices can offer live information and feedback without requiring that the user look away and check a separate device, thus increasing efficiency.

2. User Engagement - AR systems can present information in a much more involved fashion, opening possibilities for fields such as education and commercial marketing.

3. Connectivity - Users can always remain connected to the world, with information sources providing updates right before their eyes.

Concerns of Augmented Reality

Although there are many benefits to augmented reality, the risks and safety concerns associated with it also need to be heavily addressed. Some examples of potential risk include:

1. User Distraction - Abundant amounts of confusing images in a user’s field of vision can be distracting and cause potential safety concerns. For example, walking on a busy street with GPS visuals can be very dangerous and cause pedestrian accidents.

2. Potential for Hacking - The security of AR systems has not fully been addressed yet. The threat of hackers breaching AR systems could result in privacy invasions and security risks.

3. Health Concerns - There are multiple studies that have showed negative effects of augmented reality on the brain, suggesting a decrease in user concentration.
